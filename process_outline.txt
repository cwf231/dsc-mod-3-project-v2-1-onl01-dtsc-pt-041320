1. Business Understanding
        Do you want to emphasize affordability, investment, or something else? 
        This framing will help you choose which stakeholder claims to address.
    - Objectives: 
        What questions are you trying to answer, and for whom?
    - Plan:
        Formal project management?
    - Success Criteria: 
        What does a successful project look like?
        How will you know when you've achieved it?
        
2. Data Understanding
    - Do you understand what the columns mean?
    - How will you select the subset of relevant data?
    - What cleaning is required?
    (Visualizations)
    
3. Data Preparation
    - Document any data you choose to drop or otherwise exclude.
    
4. Modeling
    - Testing, cross-validation, regularization.
    - What kind of model to build?
    (LogReg, Trees, Bagging/Boosting, etc)
    
5. Evaluation
    - Check back to success criteria.
    
6. Deployment
    - How did you pick the question(s) that you did?
    - Why are these questions important from a business perspective?
    - How did you decide on the data cleaning options you performed?
    - Why did you choose a given method or library?
    - Why did you select those visualizations and what did you learn from each of them?
    - Why did you pick those features as predictors?
    - How would you interpret the results?
    - How confident are you in the predictive quality of the results?
    - What are some of the things that could cause the results to be wrong?
